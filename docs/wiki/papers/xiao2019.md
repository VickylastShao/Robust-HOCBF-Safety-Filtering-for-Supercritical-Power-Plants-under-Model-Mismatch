## One-Line Summary

Extends control barrier functions (CBFs) to high-order CBFs (HOCBFs) that handle constraints with arbitrary relative degree by recursively composing class-K functions through a psi-chain, with a penalty-based method to manage feasibility conflicts with control limits.

## Problem Setting

**System model.** Affine control nonlinear system:

$$\dot{x} = f(x) + g(x)u, \quad x \in \mathbb{R}^n,\; u \in \mathcal{U} \subset \mathbb{R}^q$$

where $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$ and $g: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^{n \times q}$ are locally Lipschitz. Solutions are forward complete.

**Uncertainty model.** No explicit uncertainty or disturbance model. The method is deterministic and assumes perfect knowledge of $f$, $g$, and the constraint function $b$.

**Control objective.** Given a safety constraint $b(x) \geq 0$ with relative degree $m$ (i.e., the control $u$ first appears in the $m$-th time derivative of $b$), find a control input that renders the safe set forward invariant while optimizing a quadratic cost. A CLF can be combined with the HOCBF constraint to also enforce convergence.

## Key Contributions

1. **High-Order Barrier Function (HOBF)** for autonomous systems: defines a recursive psi-chain of class-K functions and proves that satisfaction of the final chain inequality implies forward invariance of the intersection of all intermediate sets $C_1 \cap C_2 \cap \cdots \cap C_m$.
2. **High-Order Control Barrier Function (HOCBF)** for control-affine systems: extends HOBF by requiring the control input to appear in the last stage of the chain, yielding a single linear-in-control constraint that guarantees forward invariance.
3. **Generality over exponential CBFs (Nguyen & Sreenath 2016)**: shows that setting all class-K functions to linear functions $\alpha_i(r) = k_i r$ recovers exactly the exponential CBF formulation from [12] (input-output linearization approach), making HOCBF a strict generalization.
4. **Penalty-based feasibility management**: introduces tunable penalties $p_1, \ldots, p_m > 0$ on the class-K functions to soften HOCBF constraints when they conflict with control limitations, avoiding infeasibility without requiring complex minimum-braking-distance calculations.
5. **HOCBF-QP formulation**: integrates HOCBF constraints with CLF constraints and control limits into a sequence of quadratic programs solved at each time step.

## Core Formulation

### The psi-chain (general, time-varying)

For a $m$-th order differentiable function $b: \mathbb{R}^n \times [t_0, \infty) \to \mathbb{R}$, define:

$$\psi_0(x,t) := b(x,t)$$
$$\psi_1(x,t) := \dot{\psi}_0(x,t) + \alpha_1(\psi_0(x,t))$$
$$\vdots$$
$$\psi_m(x,t) := \dot{\psi}_{m-1}(x,t) + \alpha_m(\psi_{m-1}(x,t))$$

where $\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \ldots, \alpha_m$ are (differentiable) class-$\mathcal{K}$ functions.

The associated sets are:

$$C_1(t) := \{x \in \mathbb{R}^n : \psi_0(x,t) \geq 0\}$$
$$C_2(t) := \{x \in \mathbb{R}^n : \psi_1(x,t) \geq 0\}$$
$$\vdots$$
$$C_m(t) := \{x \in \mathbb{R}^n : \psi_{m-1}(x,t) \geq 0\}$$

### HOCBF constraint for general $m$ (Eq. 14, time-varying)

A function $b$ is a HOCBF of relative degree $m$ for system $\dot{x} = f(x) + g(x)u$ if there exist differentiable class-$\mathcal{K}$ functions $\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_m$ such that:

$$L_g L_f^{m-1} b(x,t)\, u + L_f^m b(x,t) + \frac{\partial^m b(x,t)}{\partial t^m} + O(b(x,t)) + \alpha_m(\psi_{m-1}(x,t)) \geq 0$$

for all $(x,t) \in C_1(t) \cap C_2(t) \cap \cdots \cap C_m(t) \times [t_0, \infty)$, where $O(\cdot)$ denotes the remaining Lie derivatives along $f$ and partial derivatives with respect to $t$ with degree less than or equal to $m-1$ (i.e., all the lower-order terms from expanding the psi-chain that do not multiply $u$).

### Time-invariant HOCBF constraint (Eq. 18)

For time-invariant constraints $b(x) \geq 0$, the constraint simplifies to:

$$-L_g L_f^{m-1} b(x)\, u \leq L_f^m b(x) + O(b(x)) + \alpha_m(\psi_{m-1}(x))$$

for all $x \in C_1 \cap C_2 \cap \cdots \cap C_m$.

### Special case: $m=2$ with linear class-$\mathcal{K}$ functions $\alpha_i(r) = k_i \cdot r$

With $\alpha_1(r) = k_1 r$ and $\alpha_2(r) = k_2 r$ (where $k_1 > 0$, $k_2 > 0$), the psi-chain becomes:

$$\psi_0(x) = b(x)$$
$$\psi_1(x) = \dot{b}(x) + k_1 b(x) = L_f b(x) + k_1 b(x)$$

Note: for $m=2$, the control $u$ does not appear in $\psi_1$ (since $b$ has relative degree 2, $L_g b = 0$).

$$\psi_2(x) = \dot{\psi}_1(x) + k_2 \psi_1(x)$$

Expanding:

$$\psi_2(x) = \ddot{b}(x) + k_1 \dot{b}(x) + k_2(\dot{b}(x) + k_1 b(x))$$
$$= \ddot{b}(x) + (k_1 + k_2)\dot{b}(x) + k_1 k_2 b(x)$$

Since $b$ has relative degree 2: $\ddot{b}(x) = L_f^2 b(x) + L_g L_f b(x)\, u$. The HOCBF constraint $\psi_2(x) \geq 0$ yields the explicit linear-in-$u$ inequality:

$$\boxed{-L_g L_f h(x)\, u \leq L_f^2 h(x) + (k_1 + k_2) L_f h(x) + k_1 k_2 h(x)}$$

where we use $h$ to denote the constraint function (analogous to $b$). This is the canonical HOCBF-2 constraint used in many subsequent works.

### The $S$ function (Eq. 14 / 18)

The paper defines the function $S$ implicitly through the right-hand side of the HOCBF constraint. For the time-invariant case, let:

$$S(x) := L_f^m b(x) + O(b(x)) + \alpha_m(\psi_{m-1}(x))$$

Then the HOCBF constraint (Eq. 18) reads:

$$-L_g L_f^{m-1} b(x)\, u \leq S(x)$$

For $m=2$ with linear class-$\mathcal{K}$: $S(x) = L_f^2 h(x) + (k_1+k_2) L_f h(x) + k_1 k_2 h(x)$.

### Penalty-augmented psi-chain (Eq. 21)

To manage feasibility conflicts with control limits, penalties $p_1, \ldots, p_m > 0$ are introduced:

$$\psi_1(x) := \dot{\psi}_0(x) + p_1 \alpha_1(\psi_0(x))$$
$$\psi_2(x) := \dot{\psi}_1(x) + p_2 \alpha_2(\psi_1(x))$$
$$\vdots$$
$$\psi_m(x) := \dot{\psi}_{m-1}(x) + p_m \alpha_m(\psi_{m-1}(x))$$

Smaller penalties weaken the HOCBF constraint, reducing control demand when the constraint is active, at the cost of slower convergence of the barrier function to the safe set boundary.

## Theoretical Results

**Theorem 4 (HOBF, autonomous systems).** The set $C_1(t) \cap C_2(t) \cap \cdots \cap C_m(t)$ is forward invariant for system $\dot{x} = f(x)$ if $b(x,t)$ is a HOBF (i.e., $\psi_m(x,t) \geq 0$ for all $(x,t)$ in the intersection of sets).

*Proof sketch.* By Lemma 1 (comparison lemma): $\psi_m \geq 0$ implies $\dot{\psi}_{m-1} + \alpha_m(\psi_{m-1}) \geq 0$, so $\psi_{m-1} \geq 0$ for all time (given $\psi_{m-1}(t_0) \geq 0$). Iterate backward through the chain.

**Theorem 5 (HOCBF, control systems).** Given a HOCBF $b(x,t)$ with associated sets $C_1(t), \ldots, C_m(t)$, if $x(t_0) \in C_1(t_0) \cap \cdots \cap C_m(t_0)$, then any Lipschitz continuous controller $u(t) \in \mathcal{U}$ that satisfies the HOCBF constraint (Eq. 14) for all $t \geq t_0$ renders the set $C_1(t) \cap C_2(t) \cap \cdots \cap C_m(t)$ forward invariant for system $\dot{x} = f(x) + g(x)u$.

**Feasibility conditions (Sec. III-E).** The HOCBF constraint (Eq. 18) may conflict with control limits $u_{\min} \leq u \leq u_{\max}$. When $L_g L_f^{m-1} b(x) < 0$:

$$-L_g L_f^{m-1} b(x(t))\, u_{\min} \leq -L_g L_f^{m-1} b(x(t))\, u(t)$$

so the HOCBF constraint is feasible when $S(x) \geq -L_g L_f^{m-1} b(x)\, u_{\min}$. If not, the QP is infeasible. The penalty mechanism (smaller $p_i$) reduces the right-hand side $S(x)$, making feasibility more likely, but the initial conditions $\psi_i(x(t_0)) \geq 0$ must still hold.

**Remark 2 (Initial condition feasibility).** If $b(x(t_0),t_0) \geq 0$, one can always choose class-$\mathcal{K}$ functions to ensure $\psi_1(x(t_0),t_0) \geq 0, \ldots, \psi_{m-1}(x(t_0),t_0) \geq 0$. However, extreme cases where $b(t_0) = 0$ and $\dot{b}(t_0) < 0$ make $\psi_1(t_0)$ always negative regardless of $\alpha_1$. Such cases require feasibility enforcement methods beyond the scope of this paper.

**Remark 4 (Equivalence to exponential CBF).** Setting $\alpha_i(r) = k_i r$ with $k_i > 0$ recovers exactly the exponential CBF formulation of Nguyen & Sreenath (2016) obtained through input-output linearization.

## Algorithm / Implementation

### HOCBF-QP formulation

Time is discretized into intervals of length $\Delta t$. At each time step $t = t_0 + \omega \Delta t$ ($\omega = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$), assuming constant control over the interval, the following QP is solved:

$$u^*(t) = \arg\min_{u(t)} \frac{1}{2} u(t)^\top H\, u(t) + F^\top u(t)$$

subject to:

1. **HOCBF constraint(s)** (Eq. 18, linear in $u$ at fixed state)
2. **CLF constraint** (relaxed with slack $\delta$): $L_g V(x)\, u \leq -L_f V(x) - \epsilon V(x) + \delta$
3. **Control limits**: $u_{\min} \leq u \leq u_{\max}$
4. **Additional HOCBF constraints** for other relative-degree-1 constraints (e.g., speed limits)

The optimal control $u^*(t)$ is applied and held constant for the interval, then the dynamics are integrated and the procedure repeats.

### Pseudocode (ACC example from paper)

```
Input: dynamics f,g; constraint b(x)>=0 with rel. degree m;
       class-K functions alpha_1,...,alpha_m; penalties p_1,...,p_m;
       CLF V; time step dt; horizon [t0, tf]

Initialize x(t0)
for omega = 0, 1, 2, ... do
    t = t0 + omega * dt
    Compute psi_0, psi_1, ..., psi_m at current x(t)
    Form HOCBF constraint: -L_g L_f^{m-1} b * u <= S(x)
    Form CLF constraint: L_g V * u <= -L_f V - eps*V + delta
    Form control limit constraints: u_min <= u <= u_max
    Solve QP for u*(t)
    Apply u*(t) over [t, t+dt]
    Integrate dynamics to get x(t+dt)
end for
```

## Limitations

1. **No robustness to disturbances or model uncertainty**: the formulation assumes exact knowledge of $f$, $g$, and $b$. No disturbance or noise model is considered.
2. **Feasibility is not guaranteed**: the HOCBF constraint can conflict with control limits, leading to infeasible QPs. The penalty mechanism helps but does not provide hard feasibility guarantees.
3. **Initial condition requirement**: forward invariance requires $x(t_0) \in C_1 \cap C_2 \cap \cdots \cap C_m$. If $\psi_i(x(t_0)) < 0$ for some $i$, safety cannot be guaranteed. Extreme cases (e.g., $b(t_0) = 0$, $\dot{b}(t_0) < 0$) are inherently infeasible.
4. **Sign assumption on $L_g L_f^{m-1} b$**: the feasibility analysis (Sec. III-E) assumes $L_g L_f^{m-1} b(x)$ does not change sign. If it does, the feasibility argument breaks down.
5. **Class-$\mathcal{K}$ function selection is heuristic**: the choice of $\alpha_i$ (linear, quadratic, etc.) and penalties $p_i$ significantly affects behavior, but there is no systematic method for tuning them.
6. **Conservatism of penalty approach**: reducing penalties to avoid infeasibility weakens safety enforcement, potentially allowing slower convergence or near-violations.
7. **Single constraint per HOCBF**: each constraint $b(x) \geq 0$ requires its own HOCBF and psi-chain. Interactions between multiple HOCBF constraints are not analyzed.

## Relevance to RoCBF-Net

**Phase: CBF backbone / safety filter.** The HOCBF formulation provides the foundational CBF constraint structure that RoCBF-Net likely builds upon or robustifies.

**Formulas adopted:**
- The **psi-chain definition** (Eq. 11) and the **general HOCBF constraint** (Eq. 14/18) form the backbone of the safety constraint in the control pipeline.
- The **$m=2$ linear case** $-L_g L_f h\, u \leq L_f^2 h + (k_1+k_2) L_f h + k_1 k_2 h$ is the most commonly instantiated form for relative-degree-2 safety constraints (e.g., collision avoidance with double-integrator or higher-order dynamics).
- The **$S$ function** $S(x) = L_f^m b(x) + O(b(x)) + \alpha_m(\psi_{m-1}(x))$ defines the right-hand side of the HOCBF constraint and is the quantity that RoCBF-Net may need to robustify against uncertainty.

**What we modify:**
- RoCBF-Net likely addresses the **lack of robustness** (Limitation 1) by learning or adapting the class-$\mathcal{K}$ functions, the $S$ function, or the entire HOCBF constraint under uncertainty --- something the original HOCBF framework does not handle.
- The **penalty-based feasibility management** may be replaced or augmented by a learned feasibility-aware formulation.
- The **heuristic class-$\mathcal{K}$ tuning** (Limitation 5) is a natural target for a neural network to automate.
