## One-Line Summary
Embeds the ECBF-QP as a differentiable optimization layer within a neural network that learns environment-dependent class-K gains ($K_\alpha$), enabling safety-critical controllers to generalize to novel environments with forward invariance guarantees.

## Problem Setting

- **System model:** Nonlinear control affine system
  $$\dot{x} = f(x) + g(x)u, \quad x \in \mathcal{X} \subset \mathbb{R}^n, \; u \in \mathbb{R}^m$$
  where $f$ and $g$ are locally Lipschitz continuous. Demonstrated on 2D double integrator ($n=4$, relative degree $r=2$) and 2D quadruple integrator ($n=8$, relative degree $r=4$).

- **Uncertainty model:** No parametric model uncertainty is assumed. The "uncertainty" is the environment: obstacle locations, shapes, and orientations are sampled from a distribution $\mathcal{P}_e$ and are not known a priori. A fixed $K_\alpha$ tuned for one environment may cause large detours or even safety violations in another.

- **Control objective:** Find a policy $\Pi_\theta(u_\text{perf}, e, x_0)$ that minimizes an episodic performance cost over trajectory $\tau$:
  $$\arg\min_\theta \; \mathbb{E}_{x_0 \sim \mathcal{P}_0,\, e \sim \mathcal{P}_e} \bigl[ \mathcal{L}(\tau, e, x_0) \bigr] \quad \text{s.t.} \quad u = \Pi_\theta(u_\text{perf}, e, x_0), \; \dot{x} = f(x)+g(x)u$$
  The performance cost is the sum of squared distances to the goal: $\mathcal{L}_\text{perf} = \sum_{t=0}^{T} \|x_t - x_\text{goal}\|^2$.

## Key Contributions

1. **Differentiable ECBF-QP framework:** Integrates the ECBF-QP as a differentiable convex optimization layer (via cvxpylayers [1]) within a deep learning architecture, enabling end-to-end gradient-based training of environment-dependent safety-critical controllers.
2. **Alpha-net ($\alpha$-net):** Introduces a neural network that outputs the class-K gain vector $K_\alpha$ (encoded as eigenvalues $p_i$) conditioned on environment information $e$ and initial state $x_0$, replacing manual tuning.
3. **Forward invariance guarantee:** Proves (Thm. 2) that the $\alpha$-net structure guarantees $p_i$ satisfies the ECBF validity conditions of Thm. 1, ensuring the safe set $C_0$ remains forward invariant for any learned $\theta$.
4. **Empirical generalization:** Demonstrates generalization to novel single-obstacle and multi-obstacle environments on double and quadruple integrator systems, with performance close to environment-specific optimal $K_\alpha$.

## Core Formulation

### Exponential Control Barrier Function (ECBF)

For a relative-degree-$r$ function $h: \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R}$ with safe set $C = \{x : h(x) \geq 0\}$, $h$ is an ECBF if there exists a row vector $K_\alpha \in \mathbb{R}^r$ such that:
$$\sup_{u \in \mathbb{R}^m} \bigl[ L_f^r h(x) + L_g L_f^{r-1} h(x) u \bigr] \geq -K_\alpha \,\bar{\eta}(x)$$
where $\bar{\eta}(x) = [h(x),\; \dot{h}(x),\; \ddot{h}(x),\; \ldots,\; h^{(r-1)}(x)]^\top = [h(x),\; L_f h(x),\; L_f^2 h(x),\; \ldots,\; L_f^{r-1} h(x)]^\top$.

The dynamics of $h$ are cast as a linear system:
$$\dot{\bar{\eta}}(x) = F\,\bar{\eta}(x) + G\,\mu, \quad h(x) = C\,\bar{\eta}(x)$$
where $\mu = L_f^r h(x) + L_g L_f^{r-1} h(x) u$, and $F \in \mathbb{R}^{r \times r}$, $G \in \mathbb{R}^{r \times 1}$, $C \in \mathbb{R}^{1 \times r}$ are the canonical controllable-form matrices.

### ECBF-QP

$$u^*(x) = \arg\min_{u \in \mathbb{R}^m} \|u - u_\text{perf}\|^2 \quad \text{s.t.} \quad L_f^r h(x) + L_g L_f^{r-1} h(x) u \geq -K_\alpha \,\bar{\eta}(x)$$

### The $\alpha$-net: Learning Environment-Dependent Class-K Gains

Rather than hand-tuning $K_\alpha$, the paper encodes it via eigenvalues $p_1, \ldots, p_r$ of $(F - GK_\alpha)$. The $\alpha$-net computes:

$$\alpha_\theta(e, x_0, \bar{\eta}(x)) = \prod_{i=1}^{r} \bigl[L_f + p_i(e, x_0; \theta)\bigr] \circ h(x) \;-\; L_f^r h(x)$$

This is equivalent to $K_\alpha \bar{\eta}(x)$ by the relationship between the characteristic polynomial and the companion-form matrix (cf. Remark 2). The eigenvalue network is:

$$p(e, x_0; \theta) = \text{ReLU}\!\Bigl(\prod_{k=0}^{m} \sigma(W_k l_k) - b(x_0)\Bigr) + b(x_0)$$

where:
- $l_0 = [e, x_0]$ (concatenation of environment info and initial state)
- $b_i(x_0) = \text{ReLU}\!\bigl(-\frac{\dot{v}_{i-1}(x_0)}{v_{i-1}(x_0)} - \epsilon\bigr) + \epsilon$, for $i = 1, \ldots, r$
- $\epsilon > 0$ is a small positive constant

The bias $b(x_0)$ encodes the minimum required value of each $p_i$ from Thm. 1 (Theorem 1 in the paper, from [23, Thm. 2]): $p_i$ must satisfy $p_i > 0$ and $p_i \geq -\frac{\dot{v}_{i-1}(x_0)}{v_{i-1}(x_0)}$.

### Differentiable ECBF-QP with Slack

$$\Pi_\theta(u_\text{perf}, e, x_0) = \arg\min_{u \in \mathbb{R}^m,\, \delta \in \mathbb{R}} \|u - u_\text{perf}\|^2 + \zeta \delta^2 \quad \text{s.t.} \quad L_f^r h(x) + L_g L_f^{r-1} h(x) u \geq -\alpha_\theta(e, x_0, \bar{\eta}(x)) - \delta^2$$

- $\delta$ is a slack variable ensuring feasibility during training; $\zeta$ is a hyperparameter.
- $\delta$ is **not** used at test time.
- The QP is embedded as a differentiable layer using cvxpylayers (Agrawal et al., 2019 [1]), enabling backpropagation through the optimization.

### End-to-End Differentiable Pipeline

1. Environment $e$ and initial state $x_0$ are fed to the $\alpha$-net, which outputs eigenvalues $[p_1, \ldots, p_r]$.
2. These eigenvalues construct $\alpha_\theta(e, x_0, \bar{\eta}(x)) = K_\alpha \bar{\eta}(x)$.
3. $\alpha_\theta$ parameterizes the ECBF constraint in the QP.
4. The differentiable QP layer (cvxpylayers) solves for $u^*$.
5. $u^*$ is applied to the dynamics $\dot{x} = f(x) + g(x)u$, producing trajectory $\tau$.
6. Performance loss $\mathcal{L}(\tau, e, x_0)$ is backpropagated through the entire pipeline, including through the QP solution, to update $\theta$.

## Theoretical Results

### Theorem 1 (from [23, Thm. 2])

A valid exponential CBF satisfies two conditions: if $K_\alpha$ is chosen such that $p_i > 0$ and the eigenvalues $-p_i$ satisfy:
$$p_i \geq -\frac{\dot{v}_{i-1}(x_0)}{v_{i-1}(x_0)}$$
then the ECBF constraint (9b) guarantees $h(x)$ is an exponential control barrier function, and the set $C_0 = \{x : h(x) \geq 0\}$ is forward invariant.

### Theorem 2 (Forward invariance of learned $\alpha$-net)

Given the function $p(e, x_0; \theta)$ defined in (13), each $p_i$ satisfies the conditions in Theorem 1 for **any** learned $\theta$. Thus $h(x)$ remains an exponential CBF and $C_0$ is forward invariant.

**Proof sketch:** Since $b_i(x_0) = \max\{-\frac{\dot{v}_{i-1}(x_0)}{v_{i-1}(x_0)}, \epsilon\}$ and $p_i(e, x_0, \theta) \geq b_i(x_0)$ (by the ReLU + $b$ structure), we have $p_i \geq -\frac{\dot{v}_{i-1}(x_0)}{v_{i-1}(x_0)}$ and $p_i \geq \epsilon > 0$, which are exactly the Theorem 1 conditions.

**Key implication:** Safety is guaranteed by construction regardless of the neural network weights. The learning only affects performance (how close to optimal the trajectory is), never safety.

## Algorithm / Implementation

### Network Architecture

- **$\alpha$-net:** Feedforward neural network with 2 hidden layers, each of size 100.
- **Input:** $[e, x_0]$ where $e = [y_c^\top, \text{diag}(\Lambda)^\top, \theta]^\top \in \mathbb{R}^5$ encodes the ellipse obstacle (center, size, orientation).
- **Output:** $p \in \mathbb{R}^r$ (eigenvalues), from which $K_\alpha$ is constructed.
- **Initialization:** Weights randomly initialized with positive numbers.
- **Activation:** ReLU after the bias-addition structure; $\sigma(\cdot)$ is any activation for intermediate layers.

### Training Procedure (Algorithm 1)

1. **Input:** Environment distribution $\mathcal{P}_e$, initial state distribution $\mathcal{P}_0$, time interval $\Delta t$, horizon $T$.
2. **Loop** over iterations:
   - For $i = 1, \ldots, n$: sample $e_i \sim \mathcal{P}_e$, $x_{0,i} \sim \mathcal{P}_0$; collect trajectory $\tau_i$ using the current controller.
   - Update $\theta \leftarrow \theta - \frac{\lambda}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_\theta(\tau_i, e_i, x_{0,i})$.
3. **Output:** Learned weights $\theta$.

### Loss Function

$$\mathcal{L}(\tau, e, x_0) = \mathcal{L}_\text{perf}(\tau, e, x_0) + \lambda_\delta \sum_{t=1}^{T} \delta_t^2$$

where:
- $\mathcal{L}_\text{perf} = \sum_{t=0}^{T} \|x_t - x_\text{goal}\|^2$ (goal-reaching cost)
- $\lambda_\delta$ penalizes slack variable activation (encourages feasibility without slack)
- $\delta_t$ is the slack at time step $t$

### Training Hyperparameters

- 100 training iterations; 30 environments sampled per iteration; 8s simulation per rollout.
- Performance controller: LQR.

### Multiple Obstacles Extension (Eq. 15)

At test time, the same learned $\alpha$-net is applied independently per obstacle:

$$\Pi_\theta(u_\text{perf}, e, x_0) = \arg\min_{u \in \mathbb{R}^m} \|u - u_\text{perf}\|^2 \quad \text{s.t.} \quad L_f^r h_j(x) + L_g L_f^{r-1} h_j(x) u \geq -\alpha_\theta(e_j, x_0, \bar{\eta}_j(x)), \quad j = 1, \ldots, m$$

### Obstacle Representation

Obstacles are ellipses: $h(y) = (y - y_c)^\top Q (y - y_c) - 1$, where $y = Cx$, $Q = R(\theta)^\top \Lambda R(\theta)$, and $e = [y_c, \text{diag}(\Lambda)^\top, \theta]^\top \in \mathbb{R}^5$.

## Limitations

1. **No model uncertainty:** Assumes known dynamics $f, g$. Does not handle parametric or structural uncertainty in the system model.
2. **Assumes given performance controller:** The high-level controller $u_\text{perf}$ is fixed (LQR/CLF-QP); only $K_\alpha$ is learned. The overall performance is co-determined by $u_\text{perf}$ and the CBF filter.
3. **Limited environment representation:** Uses hand-crafted obstacle descriptors ($y_c, \Lambda, \theta$). Scaling to raw sensor inputs (images, point clouds) is mentioned as future work but not demonstrated.
4. **Episodic training only:** Requires environment sampling and full trajectory rollouts for training; no online adaptation.
5. **Slack variable at training:** Although penalized, the slack $\delta$ allows safety constraint violation during training; the paper does not formally bound training-time constraint violations.
6. **Scalability to many obstacles:** Each obstacle adds an ECBF constraint; computational cost grows linearly but no study of scaling beyond 2 obstacles.
7. **Linear class-K only:** Restricts to linear class-K functions (i.e., $K_\alpha \bar{\eta}$ rather than general nonlinear $\alpha$); this limits expressiveness but enables the eigenvalue-based construction.
8. **Small-scale validation:** Only demonstrated on 2D double/quadruple integrators with simple elliptical obstacles.

## Relevance to RoCBF-Net

### Applicable Phase
- **Phase 2 (differentiable CBF-QP training):** The differentiable QP layer and the concept of learning class-K parameters via backpropagation through the safety filter are directly applicable.

### What We Adopt

1. **Differentiable QP layer using cvxpylayers / optimistix:** The core idea of embedding the CBF-QP as a differentiable layer. For RoCBF-Net we may use `optimistix` or `cvxpylayers` to enable gradient flow through the QP solve, allowing end-to-end training of safety-critical controllers.

2. **Learnable class-K parameters:** The $\alpha$-net concept — making the class-K gain $K_\alpha$ (or its equivalent eigenvalue parameterization) a learned, environment-conditioned function — is a key building block. RoCBF-Net can adopt this for learning robust/uncertainty-aware class-K parameters.

3. **Structured output for safety guarantees:** The ReLU + bias trick to enforce $p_i \geq b_i(x_0) > 0$ by construction, ensuring forward invariance regardless of learned weights, is a design pattern we should replicate for any learned safety parameters.

4. **Slack variable with penalty:** The use of $\delta$ during training (with penalty $\lambda_\delta \sum \delta_t^2$) to handle temporary infeasibility is practical for training differentiable QP controllers.

### What We Modify / Extend

- RoCBF-Net operates under **model uncertainty** (GP or ensemble), which this paper does not address. We need to extend the differentiable QP framework to handle uncertain dynamics (e.g., robust/uncertainty-aware ECBF constraints).
- We may generalize from **linear class-K** to **nonlinear class-K** functions for greater expressiveness.
- The environment representation should be extended beyond hand-crafted obstacle descriptors to richer perceptual inputs.
