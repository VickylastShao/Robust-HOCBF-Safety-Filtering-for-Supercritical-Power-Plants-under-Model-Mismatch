\begin{abstract}
Safety filters based on control barrier functions (CBFs) project control actions into the safe set, but their certificate relies on accurate dynamics---a condition routinely violated in industrial plants under coal quality drift, equipment aging, and unmodeled disturbances. This paper presents RoCBF-Net, a tunable safety filtering architecture that combines three components: high-order CBF (HOCBF) constraints with correct relative degrees, Gaussian process (GP) residual learning, and a scalar robustness factor $\epsilon_\kappa$ that scales the compositional uncertainty margin in the quadratic program constraint. The key finding is that GP mean correction---not the compositional $\epsilon(x)$ margin---is the dominant safety mechanism, reducing constraint violations by $>500\times$ versus uncorrected HOCBF across eight conditions on a 5th-order supercritical boiler-turbine benchmark. Moreover, the optimal $\epsilon_\kappa$ depends systematically on the uncertainty structure: $\epsilon_\kappa=0$ suffices for additive bounded perturbations, while $\epsilon_\kappa=0.1$ is required for state-dependent coupling (recovering zero violation from 33.5\% failure). Crucially, the theoretical worst-case $\epsilon_\kappa=1.0$ is never the optimal choice under any tested condition, and a coupling-strength sweep reveals a \emph{deployment envelope}---the safe $\epsilon_\kappa$ range collapses from $[0.1,1.0]$ at weak coupling to the singleton $\{0\}$ at strong coupling ($\gamma\geq1.5$), where any positive margin harms safety. These results motivate treating $\epsilon_\kappa$ as a tunable calibration parameter selected per uncertainty type and strength, rather than a fixed theoretical constant. The safety-filter pipeline runs at ${\sim}25$\,ms per step (GPU, JIT-compiled), approximately ten times faster than the implemented SLSQP-based NMPC baseline.
\end{abstract}

\textbf{Keywords:} Control barrier functions; safety filtering; Gaussian processes; model mismatch; robustness margin calibration; deployment envelope; supercritical power plant
