\section{Related Work}

\subsection{Constrained Control and Safety Filtering}

MPC is the dominant constrained control methodology in process industries~\cite{qin2003survey,mayne2000constrained}, with offset-free variants handling steady-state model mismatch~\cite{morari1999model}. Cautious MPC~\cite{hewing2020cautious} incorporates GP uncertainty into the prediction horizon, providing chance-constrained safety at increased computational cost.

CBFs~\cite{ames2017cbf,wieland2007constructive} enforce forward invariance through QP-based control projection. HOCBFs~\cite{xiao2021hocbf} extend this to high-relative-degree constraints. Robust CBF formulations~\cite{taylor2020issf,jankovic2018robust} add margins based on structural disturbance bounds rather than data-driven estimates. A common practice across these works is to apply the full theoretical robustness margin---the worst-case bound derived from the analysis---without examining whether a smaller margin would suffice in practice. Our empirical results challenge this default: $\epsilon_\kappa=1.0$ degrades safety under all tested conditions compared to a smaller calibrated value, and the optimal margin depends systematically on both the perturbation type (additive vs.\ state-dependent) and its coupling strength.

\subsection{GP-Enhanced CBFs}

GPs provide calibrated uncertainty quantification for data-driven modeling~\cite{rasmussen2006gp,srinivas2010gp}. Several works combine GPs with CBFs~\cite{cheng2019guaranteed,fisac2018general,castaneda2021pointwise}, applying GP uncertainty at the top CBF level ($m=1$). Aali \& Liu~\cite{aali2024learning} extend GP-HOCBF integration to $m \geq 2$ by converting the chance constraint into a second-order cone program (SOCP), yielding a convex safety filter. Zhang et al.~\cite{zhang2025adaptive} propose an adaptive fast variational sparse GP with HOCBF safety filtering for real-time robot control. These concurrent works differ from ours in two respects: (i)~they apply GP uncertainty via global constraint reformulation rather than the recursive $\sigma$-chain propagation through each $\psi$-chain level, and (ii)~they target robotics and numerical simulations rather than industrial process control with DCS deployment constraints. For industrial constraints with $m\geq 2$, our compositional $\sigma$-chain propagation through the full $\psi$-chain is structurally distinct. Lederer et al.~\cite{lederer2021uniform} derive uniform GP error bounds complementary to our approach.

\subsection{Safety Margin Calibration}

A gap in the existing literature is the systematic empirical characterization of when robustness margins help versus harm. Prior work on CBF robustness margins treats the margin as a certificate to be satisfied---scaled at the worst-case bound---rather than a deployment parameter to be calibrated. We provide the first experimental demonstration that the GP-CBF robustness factor $\epsilon_\kappa$ has a \emph{deployment envelope}: a bounded regime of coupling strengths and perturbation types within which a positive margin is necessary and effective, bounded above by a regime where it becomes counterproductive. This reframes $\epsilon_\kappa$ from a theoretical constant into a commissioning-calibrated design parameter.

In the broader safety filtering landscape, shielding approaches~\cite{alshiekh2018safe} provide safety by correcting policy actions, and constrained RL methods~\cite{achiam2017constrained} enforce expected constraints. Adaptive CBFs~\cite{long2023ecbf} estimate uncertain parameters online. Our decoupled design provides a model-based, per-step probabilistic certificate compatible with any upstream controller.
