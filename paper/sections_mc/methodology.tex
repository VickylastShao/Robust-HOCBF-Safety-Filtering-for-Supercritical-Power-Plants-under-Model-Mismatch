\section{RoCBF-Net Safety-Filter Architecture}
\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Industrial Safety-Filter Workflow}

This section first describes the online safety-filter implementation and then gives the probabilistic margin certificate that supports it. The experiments in Section~\ref{sec:exp} determine how much of that margin should be applied under each mismatch regime.

Figure~\ref{fig:architecture} summarizes the overlay architecture. The upstream controller produces a deviation command $v_{\mathrm{prop}}$ around the LQR-stabilized equilibrium controller. The safety filter then returns the closest command $v^*$ that satisfies the process safety constraints. The total actuator command applied to the boiler-turbine model is
\begin{equation}
    u = u_0 + K(x_0-x) + v^* ,
    \label{eq:deviation_control}
\end{equation}
where $u=[u_B,D_{fw},u_t]^\top$ denotes coal feed, feedwater flow, and turbine-valve opening. This deviation-control form is used because the CCS dynamics are stiff. The HOCBF and QP operate on the stabilized linearized dynamics, while evaluation rollouts use the nonlinear 5th-order plant with $\Phi(p_m)$-scaled control effectiveness.

\paragraph{Online filtering algorithm.}
At each sampling instant, RoCBF-Net executes the following steps:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{Step \arabic*:},leftmargin=*]
    \item Read the measured state $x_k$ and obtain the proposed deviation action $v_{\mathrm{prop},k}$ from the upstream controller.
    \item Evaluate the GP residual model at $x_k$ to obtain the mean correction $\mu_{\mathrm{GP}}(x_k)$ and posterior standard deviations $\sigma_{\mathrm{GP}}(x_k)$.
    \item Build the mean-corrected HOCBF constraints using the correct relative degree for each process limit.
    \item Propagate GP posterior uncertainty through the HOCBF $\psi$-chain to compute the compositional margin $\epsilon(x_k)$.
    \item Solve the real-time QP
    \begin{equation}
        v_k^*=\argmin_v \|v-v_{\mathrm{prop},k}\|_2^2
        \quad \mathrm{s.t.}\quad A(x_k)v \leq b(x_k)-\epsilon_\kappa\epsilon(x_k),
        \label{eq:online_qp}
    \end{equation}
    with actuator box constraints and row scaling.
    \item Apply $u_k=u_0+K(x_0-x_k)+v_k^*$ to the plant, and log intervention, feasibility, and constraint-margin diagnostics.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Operating-point interpretation.}
RoCBF-Net denotes the full safety-filter architecture in Fig.~\ref{fig:architecture}: HOCBF constraints with the correct relative degrees, GP residual mean correction, compositional uncertainty propagation, and a tunable scalar $\epsilon_\kappa$. The experimental rows labelled GP-HOCBF, RHOCBF, and online GP-RHOCBF are fixed operating points or ablations of this architecture, not separate proposed methods. Thus, $\epsilon_\kappa=0$ tests mean correction without an additional uncertainty margin, $\epsilon_\kappa=1$ tests the full worst-case margin, and the recommended operating point is selected from the commissioning schedule in Secs.~\ref{sec:kappa}--\ref{sec:envelope}.

\subsection{Problem Formulation}

Consider a control-affine system with model mismatch:
\begin{equation}
    \dot{x} = \underbrace{f_0(x) + g_0(x)u}_{\text{nominal}} + \underbrace{\Deltaf(x)}_{\text{residual}}, \quad x \in \R^n, \; u \in \calU \subset \R^m
    \label{eq:true}
\end{equation}
where $\Deltaf$ represents unmodeled dynamics, parameter drift, and external disturbances. We assume that the input matrix is known ($\Delta g = 0$), consistent with settings in which actuator characteristics are well characterized. In scenarios where actuator degradation mainly affects process variables through reduced throughput (for example, valve fouling reducing steam extraction efficiency) rather than through actuator-gain changes, the dominant effect is captured by $\Deltaf$. The S5 benchmark scenario models actuator degradation in this form.

For a constraint $h(x) \geq 0$ with relative degree $m$, the HOCBF $\psi$-chain is:
\begin{equation}
    \psi_0 = h(x), \quad \psi_i = \dot{\psi}_{i-1} + \alpha_i(\psi_{i-1}), \;\; i=1,\ldots,m
    \label{eq:psi}
\end{equation}
With linear $\alpha_i(r) = k_i r$, the constraint $A_0(x)u \leq b_0(x)$ enforces forward invariance when the model is exact.

\subsection{GP Residual Model}

Each component of $\Deltaf_j$ is modeled as an independent GP with a Mat\'{e}rn-5/2 kernel. Given $N$ training points, the posterior provides mean $\mu_{\mathrm{GP},j}(x)$ and variance $\sigma_{\mathrm{GP},j}^2(x)$. The GP-UCB bound gives:
\begin{equation}
    |\Deltaf_j(x) - \mu_{\mathrm{GP},j}(x)| \leq \beta \sigma_{\mathrm{GP},j}(x), \quad \text{w.p.} \geq 1-\delta/n
    \label{eq:gp_bound}
\end{equation}
with $\beta = \sqrt{2(\gamma_N + 1 + \ln(n/\delta))}$ and maximum information gain $\gamma_N$~\cite{srinivas2010gp}.

We define the mean-corrected drift $\fhat = f_0 + \muGP$ and posterior residual $\Delta \hat{f} = \Deltaf - \muGP$.

\subsection{Compositional Robustness Margin}

The perturbation at level $i$ is $\delta_i = \psi_i - \hat{\psi}_i^0$, satisfying:
\begin{align}
    \delta_1 &= L_{\Delta\hat{f}}h \label{eq:d1}\\
    \delta_i &= L_{\Delta\hat{f}}\hat{\psi}_{i-1}^0 + (L_{\fhat}+k_i)\delta_{i-1} + L_{\Delta\hat{f}}\delta_{i-1}, \; i\geq 2 \label{eq:di}
\end{align}

The compositional margin is constructed as:
\begin{align}
    \sigma_1(x) &= \beta \sum_j |\partial h/\partial x_j| \sigmaGPj{j}(x) \label{eq:s1}\\
    \sigma_i(x) &= \beta \sum_j |\partial \hat{\psi}_{i-1}^0/\partial x_j| \sigmaGPj{j}(x) + (\|L_{\fhat}\|_{\mathrm{op}} + k_i)\sigma_{i-1}(x) + \sigma_{\mathrm{cross}}^{(i)}(x) \label{eq:si}\\
    \sigmatotal(x) &= \sigma_m(x) + \sum_{j=1}^{m-1} c_j\sigma_j(x) + \sigma_{\mathrm{ctrl}}(x) \label{eq:st}
\end{align}
Here, $\|L_{\fhat}\|_{\mathrm{op}}$ is the operator norm (the spectral norm of the Jacobian $\partial \fhat / \partial x$ at the equilibrium operating point), $\sigma_{\mathrm{cross}}^{(i)}(x)$ bounds the cross term $|L_{\Delta\hat{f}}\delta_{i-1}|$ via Lemma~S1, and $c_j = \prod_{i=j+1}^{m-1} (\|L_{\fhat}\|_{\mathrm{op}} + k_i)$ are the chain coupling weights. The resulting HOCBF constraint is $A_0(x)u \leq b_0(x) - \epsilonKappa\cdot\sigmatotal(x)$.

\subsection{Theoretical Guarantee: Worst-Case Bound}

\begin{theorem}[Probabilistic forward invariance]
\label{thm:1}
Suppose that the input matrix is exact ($\Delta g = 0$), each GP residual component satisfies the RKHS boundedness condition required by the GP-UCB confidence bound, the GP posterior is calibrated on fixed i.i.d.\ training data, the relative degree is known, the residual dynamics are sufficiently small and Lipschitz on the operating set, and the robust QP is feasible. For $\epsilonKappa = 1$, if $\epsilon(x) \geq |\Delta(x,u)|$ for all admissible $u$, then $\calC$ is forward invariant with probability at least $1-\delta$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
\textbf{Step 1 (GP calibration):} By the RKHS boundedness and GP calibration conditions together with~\eqref{eq:gp_bound}, $|\Delta\hat{f}_j| \leq \beta\sigmaGPj{j}$ holds with probability $\geq 1-\delta/n$ per dimension; by union bound over the $n$ state dimensions, this holds simultaneously with probability $\geq 1-\delta$.

\textbf{Step 2 (Compositional bound):} By induction, $|\delta_i| \leq \sigma_i$. The cross term $\sigma_{\mathrm{cross}}^{(i)}$ is bounded via Lemma~S1 (Appendix): $|L_{\Delta\hat{f}}\delta_{i-1}| \leq G_\delta^{(i-1)} \beta\|\sigma_{\mathrm{GP}}\|_2$.

\textbf{Step 3:} $|\Delta(x,u)| \leq \sigmatotal(x) = \epsilon(x)$ for $\epsilonKappa=1$, completing the certificate.
\end{proof}

Theorem~\ref{thm:1} is a \emph{worst-case} statement. Setting $\epsilon_\kappa=1$ applies the full compositional margin used in the certificate, so forward invariance holds with the stated probability whenever the tightened QP remains feasible under the actuator authority condition. Feasibility is not implied by the margin itself; it depends on the safe-set geometry, actuator limits, and feasible-control set $\calU$. The guarantee is conservative because it covers the worst-case combination of GP posterior uncertainty and $\psi$-chain amplification. The next subsection treats $\epsilon_\kappa$ as a tunable parameter that recovers this bound at $\epsilon_\kappa=1$ while permitting less conservative calibration for specific uncertainty regimes.

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{$\epsilon_\kappa$}{epsilon-kappa} as a Tunable Safety Factor}

The robustness scaling factor $\epsilon_\kappa$ in the QP constraint $A_0(x)u \leq b_0(x) - \epsilon_\kappa\cdot\epsilon(x)$ controls the safety-margin/control-authority trade-off:

\begin{itemize}
    \item $\epsilon_\kappa = 0$: The QP enforces $A_0u \leq b_0$, i.e., the nominal HOCBF constraint with GP mean correction only. No robustness margin is applied beyond the mean correction itself.
    \item $\epsilon_\kappa = 1$: The QP enforces the full compositional margin $\epsilon(x)$, recovering the worst-case certificate of Theorem~\ref{thm:1}.
    \item $0 < \epsilon_\kappa < 1$: The QP enforces a fraction of the compositional margin, trading theoretical coverage for reduced QP conservatism.
\end{itemize}

The empirical question is whether the worst-case setting ($\epsilon_\kappa=1$) is needed in the tested process-control benchmark, and whether the preferred $\epsilon_\kappa$ changes with the structure and strength of model mismatch. Section~\ref{sec:exp} shows that: (i)~$\epsilon_\kappa=0$ (GP correction only) is sufficient for additive bounded perturbations, achieving $\leq0.18\%$ violation across six of seven uncertainty scenarios; (ii)~$\epsilon_\kappa=0.1$ is required for state-dependent coupling, recovering zero violation from 33.5\% failure; (iii)~$\epsilon_\kappa=1.0$ is not the preferred choice under any tested condition; and (iv)~the safe $\epsilon_\kappa$ range collapses as coupling strength increases, defining a deployment envelope that depends on the perturbation amplification rate $\gamma$.

\subsection{Data-Driven vs. Floor-Driven \texorpdfstring{$\epsilon(x)$}{epsilon(x)}}

The GP posterior includes a regularization floor $\sigma_{\mathrm{floor}}=10^{-4}$. Under constant perturbations, $\sigmaGP$ is near-uniform and $\epsilon(x)$ is approximately constant per constraint; the margin is \emph{floor-driven}, with the GP contributing a near-constant safety buffer. Under state-dependent perturbations (S3: Coupled), $\sigmaGP$ becomes heterogeneous and $\epsilon(x)$ is state-dependent; the margin is \emph{data-driven}, reflecting the spatial structure of uncertainty. This distinction explains why additive perturbation scenarios can use $\epsilon_\kappa=0$ in the tested regime: the GP mean correction already provides the useful adjustment, and the floor-driven $\epsilon(x)$ mainly adds QP conservatism.

\subsection{Sampled-Data Implementation}

Theorem~\ref{thm:1} is a continuous-time result. The implementation uses discrete sampling ($T_s = 1$\,s, zero-order hold). The full safety-filter pipeline latency is ${\sim}25$\,ms on the GPU after JIT compilation, including the PPO forward pass, GP inference, and QP solve. When the QP is infeasible, the controller invokes a slack-penalised relaxation with LQR fallback. Inter-sample constraint satisfaction is checked empirically in Section~\ref{sec:exp}; a formal discrete-time invariance theorem is outside the present scope.
