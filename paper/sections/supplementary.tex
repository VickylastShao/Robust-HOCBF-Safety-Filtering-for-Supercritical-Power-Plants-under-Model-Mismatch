\subsection{S1. Generic-GP and Out-of-Distribution Deployment}

The main text (Section~7) reports that scenario-specific GP training achieves 0\% CBF violation under nominal operation and the six static perturbation deployments. This supplement provides the full OOD analysis: Table~\ref{tab:S1_ood} reports CBF violation and QP intervention rates under three GP training strategies---scenario-specific, generic mixed (trained on Nominal/S1/S2/S4), and mismatched OOD (S1$\to$S3, S3$\to$S1)---using $\Phi$-scaled rollout, $\epsilonKappa = 1.0$, and 5 seeds $\times$ 500 evaluation steps.

\begin{table}[htbp]
    \centering
    \caption{Generic-GP and OOD deployment --- CBF violation rate (\%, mean $\pm$ std over 5 seeds $\times$ 500 steps).}
    \label{tab:S1_ood}
    \footnotesize
    \begin{tabular}{l l c c}
        \toprule
        Deployment & Strategy & CBF (\%) & QP (\%) \\
        \midrule
        \multirow{2}{*}{Nominal}
            & scenario-specific & $\bm{0.0 \pm 0.0}$ & $0.2$ \\
            & generic mixed     & $\bm{0.0 \pm 0.1}$ & $43.0$ \\
        \midrule
        \multirow{3}{*}{S1: Heat}
            & scenario-specific & $\bm{0.0 \pm 0.0}$ & $100.0$ \\
            & generic mixed     & $92.2 \pm 4.6$     & $7.9$ \\
            & OOD (S3$\to$S1)   & $99.9 \pm 0.1$     & $99.8$ \\
        \midrule
        \multirow{2}{*}{S2: Pressure}
            & scenario-specific & $\bm{0.0 \pm 0.0}$ & $99.8$ \\

            & scenario-specific & $\bm{0.0 \pm 0.0}$ & $99.2$ \\

            & OOD (S1$\to$S3)   & $\bm{0.0 \pm 0.0}$ & $100.0$ \\
        \midrule
        \multirow{2}{*}{S4: Nonlinear}
            & scenario-specific & $\bm{0.0 \pm 0.0}$ & $99.6$ \\

        \midrule
        \multirow{2}{*}{S5: Valve}
            & scenario-specific & $\bm{0.0 \pm 0.0}$ & $99.6$ \\
            & generic mixed     & $98.8 \pm 0.3$     & $0.2$ \\
        \midrule
        \multirow{2}{*}{S6: Fuel}

Three observations follow. (1)~Scenario-specific GP is uniformly safe but with near-saturated QP intervention under perturbation. (2)~Generic mixed GP fails under all perturbed deployments (82.7--99.7\% violation) because it averages over training scenarios, producing $\sigmaGP$ too small to trigger the safety filter---a silent calibration failure. (3)~OOD asymmetry: S1$\to$S3 achieves 0\% violation (large $\sigmaGP$ inflates $\epsilon(x)$) while S3$\to$S1 reaches 99.9\% violation (low $\sigmaGP$ despite biased mean)---$\sigmaGP$ measures spatial distance, not functional distance between perturbation structures.

\subsection{S2. Perturbation Magnitude Sweep}

Table~\ref{tab:S2_magnitude} reports the perturbation magnitude sweep on S1:Heat (3 seeds per magnitude, $\Phi$-scaled rollout, $\epsilonKappa = 1.0$). The QP intervention rate transitions smoothly from selective (3.5\% at Mag10) to saturated (100\% at Mag75), with 0\% CBF violation through Mag75. At Mag100, the perturbation exceeds the GP's modeling capacity and violation reaches 99.8\%.
