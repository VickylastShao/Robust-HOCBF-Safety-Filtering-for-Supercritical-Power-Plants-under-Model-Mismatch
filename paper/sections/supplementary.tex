\section{Evidence Base and Result Inventory}

The study uses the 5th-order Phase~5 result inventory as the primary evidence base. The root-level inventory in \texttt{results/phase5/} contains the complete fair-comparison matrix: 8 methods, 8 conditions, and 5 seeds, for 320 JSON result files. The strict inventory audit reports 320/320 matched files, no missing root-level files, no extra root-level JSON files, and no JSON parsing errors.

The older Phase~4 3rd-order results are retained only as historical and auxiliary material. Their inventory is not used as the primary M\&C evidence base because it is incomplete for the mean-only RoCBF-Net operating point on several conditions. Where Phase~4 is mentioned in the manuscript, it identifies the previously observed generic-GP and out-of-distribution failure mode rather than supporting the main 5th-order safety comparison.

These data support three practical conclusions. First, additive perturbations (S2 and S4) require no robustness margin in the tested regime; GP mean correction alone is sufficient. Second, state-dependent coupling (S3) requires a small positive margin: $\kappa=0$ fails, while $\kappa=0.1$ restores zero observed violation. Third, the full theoretical margin $\kappa=1.0$ is not the preferred empirical operating point in these scenarios and can collapse the feasible set.

The safe $\epsilon_\kappa$ interval narrows with coupling strength and collapses to $\{0\}$ once the perturbation amplification rate reaches $\gamma \geq 1.5$. In that regime, adding margin primarily tightens an already difficult QP; infeasibility becomes a diagnostic signal for fallback rather than a reason to increase $\epsilon_\kappa$.

\section{GP Calibration Diagnostics}

Table~\ref{tab:S_gp_calib} summarizes the held-out GP calibration diagnostics. A scenario-specific GP is trained from $N_{\mathrm{train}}=500$ transitions and evaluated on $N_{\mathrm{held}}=200$ held-out transitions. The empirical coverage is the fraction of held-out residual errors satisfying the GP-UCB interval $|\Delta \hat{f}_j| \leq \beta\sigma_{\mathrm{GP},j}$.

\begin{table}[htbp]
    \centering
    \caption{GP calibration diagnostics on held-out residuals. Coverage is averaged over the three modeled residual dimensions.}
    \label{tab:S_gp_calib}
    \small
    \begin{tabular}{lccc}
        \toprule
        Scenario & Mean coverage & Mean $\sigma_{\mathrm{GP}}$ & $\beta$ \\
        \midrule
        S1: Heat & 100.0\% & 0.104 & 38.15 \\
        S2: Pressure & 100.0\% & 0.142 & 49.29 \\
        S3: Coupled & 100.0\% & 0.101 & 31.84 \\
        S4: Nonlinear & 100.0\% & 0.100 & 5.73 \\
        S5: Valve & 100.0\% & 0.100 & 5.74 \\
        S6: Fuel & 100.0\% & 0.100 & 5.77 \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The GP-UCB intervals are conservative in the held-out data, so the empirical limitation observed in the main results is not lack of pointwise GP coverage. The limiting factor is how the chosen margin scale reshapes the feasible QP set under each perturbation structure.
