Three observations follow. (1)~Scenario-specific GP is uniformly safe but with near-saturated QP intervention under perturbation. (2)~Generic mixed GP fails under all perturbed deployments (82.7--99.7\% violation) because it averages over training scenarios, producing $\sigmaGP$ too small to trigger the safety filter---a silent calibration failure. (3)~OOD asymmetry: S1$\to$S3 achieves 0\% violation (large $\sigmaGP$ inflates $\epsilon(x)$) while S3$\to$S1 reaches 99.9\% violation (low $\sigmaGP$ despite biased mean)---$\sigmaGP$ measures spatial distance, not functional distance between perturbation structures.

\section{PPO-Lagrangian Baseline Results}

PPO-Lagrangian uses dual descent on a cost constraint to penalize unsafe behavior during training, providing a constrained-RL baseline. Table~\ref{tab:S6_ppo_lagr} reports its CBF violation rates on the available Phase~4 conditions. PPO-Lagrangian achieves substantially lower violation rates than the nominal PPO-HOCBF ($>95\%$ under perturbation) but does not achieve the zero-violation performance of PPO-RHOCBF ($0\%$ across all static scenarios). The Lagrangian penalty mechanism provides partial safety through reward shaping but cannot guarantee constraint satisfaction under model mismatch---the dual variable tracks average constraint cost, not worst-case per-step forward invariance.

\section{Load-Following Performance}

Load following tests the control system's ability to track an AGC (Automatic Generation Control) power schedule while maintaining safety constraints. The schedule ramps between 750\,MW and 1000\,MW at 5\,MW/min with 20\,MW regulation amplitude and 300\,s regulation period. Table~\ref{tab:S7_load_following} reports CBF violation rates under this dynamic reference trajectory.

Under load following, the nominal dynamics are exact ($\Deltaf = 0$) and the CBF constraints are only challenged by the reference trajectory changes, not by model mismatch. NMPC achieves $0\%$ violation because its offset-free disturbance estimator converges quickly on the exact model. PPO-RHOCBF and RoCBF-Net maintain near-zero violation with the fixed scenario-specific GP providing a small robustness margin. The low violation rates across all methods confirm that load following is a less challenging condition than persistent model mismatch.

\section{GP Calibration Diagnostics}

The Robust HOCBF's forward invariance guarantee (Theorem~1) relies on the GP-UCB confidence bound $|\Delta \hat{f}_j(x)| \leq \beta \sigma_{\mathrm{GP},j}(x)$ holding uniformly over the operating region. Table~\ref{tab:S8_gp_calib} reports the empirical validation of this assumption: for each of the six static perturbation scenarios, a scenario-specific GP is trained on $N_{\mathrm{train}} = 500$ transitions and evaluated on $N_{\mathrm{held}} = 200$ held-out transitions across 3 independent training seeds. The GP-UCB $\beta$ factor is computed from the maximum information gain $\gamma_N$ via the Srinivas et al.\ (2010) bound $\beta = \sqrt{2(\gamma_N + 1 + \log(n_d/\delta))}$ with $\delta = 0.01$, targeting $\geq 99\%$ per-dimension nominal coverage.

Coverage computed as fraction of held-out residual errors satisfying $|\Delta \hat{f}_j| \leq \beta \sigma_{\mathrm{GP},j}$. $\bar{\sigma}_{\mathrm{GP}}$ is the mean GP uncertainty across all held-out points.}
    \label{tab:S8_gp_calib}
    \footnotesize
    \begin{tabular}{lcccl}
        \toprule

        S2: Pressure  & 100.0$\pm$0.0 & 100.0$\pm$0.0 & 100.0$\pm$0.0 & 100.0 & 0.14 \\

Across all six scenarios and three dimensions (18 dimension--scenario pairs), the empirical coverage is 100\% in every case---the theoretical $\beta$ bound is conservative in practice. The GP-UCB $\beta$ values range from $\beta \approx 5.3$--$5.8$ for S4--S6 (where the uncertainty structure is smooth and well-captured by the Mat\'{e}rn-5/2 kernel) to $\beta \approx 31.8$--$52.1$ for S1--S3 (where the perturbation structure produces larger information gain $\gamma_N$). The mean GP uncertainty $\bar{\sigma}_{\mathrm{GP}}$ is at or near the $\sigma_{\mathrm{floor}} = 0.01$ floor for most dimensions, indicating that the GP learns the residual structure well from the training data; the pressure dimension under S2 (pressure oscillation) shows elevated $\bar{\sigma}_{\mathrm{GP}} = 0.23$, reflecting the larger residual magnitude of this perturbation on the pressure channel. This calibration confirms that the GP-UCB confidence intervals are empirically valid and that the compositional $\epsilon(x)$ propagation through the $\psi$-chain (Theorem~1) operates on a sound uncertainty basis.
